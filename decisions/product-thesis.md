# Product Thesis

## Core Thesis

Plain Redmine is not yet a better Jira in absolute terms. A curated Redmine-based stack can become a better alternative for some software teams if it closes the most visible operational gaps while preserving Redmine's structural advantages.

## Structural Advantages to Preserve

- open-source base
- self-hosting option
- lower lock-in
- lower recurring platform cost
- direct control over data and deployment
- extensibility through Ruby on Rails plugins and REST APIs

## Problems to Solve Better Than Jira

- recurring cost growth
- vendor lock-in
- forced cloud direction
- heavy administration
- plugin sprawl and ecosystem dependence
- migration difficulty
- excessive product complexity for teams that do not need enterprise depth

## Year-One Non-Goals

- full enterprise parity with Jira
- AI parity with Atlassian platform features
- advanced service management as a primary target
- reproducing the breadth of the Atlassian Marketplace
- broad multi-segment go-to-market before internal validation

## Initial Go-To-Market Logic

1. Build internal-first.
2. Validate with real use, not a demo-only environment.
3. Package only after the platform survives real internal workflows.
4. Approach software houses first, because they are more likely to care about cost, control, extensibility, and migration pragmatism.

## Strategic Product Principle

The product should compete on control, clarity, and operational sufficiency, not on total platform breadth.

## Partnership Principle

Planio and RedmineUP can accelerate delivery, but product direction should stay independent from any single partner.

## Open Questions to Resolve in Discovery

These questions do not invalidate the thesis but must be answered before any commercial packaging work begins.

**1. What is the specific persona?**
The current framing targets "software houses." This needs to be narrowed to at least one primary profile before the gap analysis is finalized: team size, process type (Scrum, Kanban, mixed), number of concurrent projects, client-facing vs. internal delivery, and the main decision-maker role. A vague persona produces a vague product.

**2. Why this over Plane, YouTrack, or GitLab Issues?**
The market for "teams leaving Jira who want control and lower cost" already has credible alternatives. Plane is open-source, self-hostable, and modern. YouTrack is well-maintained with a reasonable pricing model at smaller scales. GitLab Issues is native for GitLab shops. The thesis must include an explicit answer to why Redmine-based over these, not just why Redmine over Jira.

**3. How does this differ from Planio?**
Planio already provides hosted Redmine with commercial support, migration help, and strong community credibility. The product thesis needs a clear answer: are we competing with Planio, complementing it, using it as infrastructure, or targeting a different segment? This question should be answered in discovery before any go-to-market framing is attempted.
